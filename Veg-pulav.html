Veg Pulao Recipe

Veg Pulao is a fragrant Indian rice dish cooked with mixed vegetables and aromatic spices. It is easy to prepare and perfect for lunch or dinner.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 onion (thinly sliced)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • ½ cup mixed vegetables (carrot, peas, beans)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 cloves
  • 1 small cinnamon stick
  • 2 green cardamom pods
  • 1 green chili (slit)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves

Instructions

  1. Wash the basmati rice and soak it in water for 20 minutes. Drain and keep aside.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a pan or pressure cooker.
  3. Add bay leaf, cloves, cinnamon, and cardamom. Sauté until aromatic.
  4. Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown.
  5. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chili. Sauté for 1 minute.
  6. Add mixed vegetables and cook for 2–3 minutes.
  7. Add soaked rice, salt, and water. Mix gently.
  8. Cover and cook until rice is done and water is absorbed.
  9. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  10. Serve hot with raita or salad.
Tip: For extra flavor, you can add a few mint leaves or a squeeze of lemon juice before serving.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Veg Pulao?
Veg Pulao is a fragrant Indian rice dish made with basmati rice, mixed vegetables, and aromatic spices. It's a healthy, flavorful, and easy-to-make meal suitable for lunch or dinner.
Is this Veg Pulao recipe vegan and gluten-free?
Yes, this recipe is naturally gluten-free. To make it vegan, simply use oil instead of ghee when cooking.
Which vegetables can I add to Veg Pulao?
You can add a variety of vegetables such as carrots, green peas, green beans, potatoes, and even cauliflower, based on your preference and availability.
How long does it take to prepare and cook Veg Pulao?
Preparation takes about 20 minutes (including soaking the rice), and cooking the pulao takes another 20–25 minutes, so it’s a quick and easy meal.
What type of rice should I use for Veg Pulao?
It’s recommended to use basmati rice for Veg Pulao as it yields a fluffy, fragrant result. However, you can use other long-grain rice varieties if basmati is not available.
Can I add extra flavors to enhance the pulao?
Yes, for extra flavor, you can add a few mint leaves or a squeeze of lemon juice before serving. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves for added freshness.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is this Veg Pulao recipe different from other market recipes?

This Veg Pulao recipe is designed to be simple, healthy, and adaptable for a variety of dietary needs, using basic Indian spices and commonly available vegetables. Unlike some market recipes that use heavy masala pastes or dairy, this version keeps spices whole and offers plant-based, gluten-free, and vegan options, making it suitable for most families.

Can I make this Veg Pulao vegan and gluten-free easily?

Yes! The recipe works perfectly with oil instead of ghee for a vegan version, and all ingredients listed are naturally gluten-free. No flour or gluten-based thickeners are used, ensuring it's safe for those on gluten-free diets.

What type of rice should I use? Is basmati necessary?

Basmati rice is recommended for its aroma and long, separated grains, which enhance the pulao's texture and presentation. However, you may substitute with other long- or medium-grain non-sticky rice if needed; just adjust the water ratio and cooking time accordingly.

How does the flavor compare with more complex pulao or biryani recipes?

This recipe delivers mild, aromatic flavor from whole spices and is lighter than traditional biryanis or heavily spiced pulaos. It's ideal for those who prefer subtle tastes or are cooking for kids and elders, but the flavor can be boosted by adding mint leaves or lemon juice as suggested.

Is this recipe suitable for meal prep and lunch boxes?

Absolutely. The Veg Pulao keeps well in the refrigerator for up to 2 days and reheats nicely, making it a great option for meal prep or lunch boxes. You can chop the vegetables the previous day for faster prep in the morning.

How long does it take to cook, and can I use a pressure cooker or Instant Pot?

The recipe is designed for quick preparation—about 30 minutes total. You can cook it on a stovetop pan or use a pressure cooker to save time. For Instant Pot users, simply sauté the spices and follow the same steps; pressure cook on low for 5 minutes with a natural release for best results.
